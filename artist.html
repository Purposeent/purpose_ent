Samamtha Bio

Born and Partially raised in bedstuy, Brooklyn by a single, hardworking Trinidadian mother by the of name Carol, Samantha first realized that she had a voice people wanted to hear at age 7. The youngest of 6 children she had a very sheltered childhood and was extremely shy. The only time she would sing out loud was at home. Her older brother Brent (or as the family calls him Jomo) would play instrumentals from his sound system loudly and she'd sing along or her mother would ask her to sing one of her favorite songs "Not gon cry" by Mary J Blige. Most of her singing abilities came from mimicking what she heard on the radio. Samantha didn't actively start pursuing music until she was 9 years old in the fourth grade. In the middle of the school year Carol had relocated the family to Coney Island. Here Samantha joined the school choir at Shirley Tanyhill (p.s.288) after being encouraged by vocal teachers Ms. Sands and Mr. Peters. She learned music fundamentals and was officially introduced to the performing arts. She enjoyed being in the chorus very much. She forged new friendships, traveled to different places in New York, and became a stronger more structured singer. However her stay at 288 was short lived as she moved on to Mark Twain Junior highschool in the 6th grade. This was her introduction to a more strict world of music. The audition process was very competitive, however she made it in. Her first year of tutelage was under Mr. Postel, a fun yet all business vocal teacher. He widened his students understanding of music deeply and pushed for them to mature as vocalists, showing them to read music and sing in other languages. After Mark Twain her family relocated to Canaries and Samantha auditioned for Brooklyn High School of the Arts and within her four years there became a well rounded vocalist under the teachings of Charlene Corbett. She left High school a strong alto versed in proper vocal technique, gospel, Broadway, classical, and learning to sing in even more languages as well. She was accepted into Long Island University where she majored in music education and studied jazz under Gloria Cooper. This is where she widened her range to soprano and broke out of her shyness. Professor Cooper's Jazz ensemble had a mandatory solo assignment for each student. For many years she had been blending in and now she was tasked with standing out. During her first year in college she gave birth to her darling son Jayden. She juggled school, work, and parenting with the help of her mother and two older sisters Leslie and Summer. As she was coming into her stride as a soloist she also started songwriting for a friend by the name of Anthony (aka Ant) whom was introduced to her by a coworker. He would provide her with instrumentals and they would record with engineer/producer Timmy Hendrix. Over time her and Timmy developed their own friendship and they began working together on songs. She then started performing her written pieces at school and even winning a competition with one song. After her third year at L.I.U. she went through a rough patch at home and couldn't really cope with everything. This was affecting her school grades so she decided to step away from classes for a while. After doing so she started writing more and met her best friend Shampaine at Timmy's studio who was also a singer/songwriter. They both were introduced to Dj Uncle Tone, through Timmy, who facilitated showcases for underground artists in the Lower eastside of Manhattan. At first performing separately and eventually coming together to form the group BlacC Shadez. Shampaine was more put together and outgoing as an artist whereas Samantha, although less shy, was a bit rough around the edges and wasn't really much of a performer. Shampaine pushed Samantha and encouraged her to polish her look and "fake it til you make it" on stage. Equipped with the confidence Shampaine drilled into her head the duo performed at many showcases and open mics and Samantha became better as an all around performer with each performance. After about 3 to 4 years of performing BlacC Shadez took a hiatus, both ladies focusing on song writing and personal artist development. At that point Samantha was searching for her sound. In 2015 she teamed up with Billie Wroteit, an old highschool friend, to form "The Verdict". The Verdict was a writing team that often branched out and worked with other songwriters. Billie was a songwriter and artist far longer than Samantha so she helped Samantha how to think outside the box to conceptualize when songwriting. Working with Billie is how Samantha found her sound, an eclectic pop/rock/ alternative/soul type. Much like her personal style her music is (as Bill would say) "outside the box." Today Samantha is putting together her first project working with writers like Billie, Eli. F, and Shampaine and producers like Purpose Entertaiment and Playmore.

Elijah Freeman Bio

“No one inspired me to sing, it's just something I always did.”

For Elijah Freeman that inspiration comes from within and is melodiously vocalized quite naturally. His casual demeanor resonates with his 6’2” slender frame. His insights flow subtlety into episodes conceptualized in the aspirations and yearnings of Elli,a moniker and depiction of what and who he is as an artist. The realization of this raw talent came to light in 5th grade for Elijah where he remembers receiving accolades not only from his fellow classmates but from his teachers as well, one of which exulted that he had an amazing singing voice. Elijah wrote his first r&b song back then, titled “Be My Baby” to his childhood crush, and has gone onto winning poetry contests, and singing in gospel choirs. Elijah has since joined a group of writers that he has affiliated himself with over the years. It is through this affiliation that he has developed a work ethic to become a singer/songwriter fashioned in a style all his own, drawing and feeding off fellow songwriters that are inspired from the likes of Usher, Musiq Soulchild, India Arie, Boyz II Men and Brandy. Although Elijah highly regards these successful artists and their styles he innately draws mostly from within so that, he may stand just as uniquely amongst that list one day. Elijah finds great solace particularly in the realm of songwriting and is a soulful tenor in the booth. He has written songs recently for an up and coming Canadian pop star Adriana Lombardo who found working with Elijah unfamiliarly encouraging. His humility shows up prominently in his writing and his style is easily set apart and is continuously evolving. He has brought this awe inspiring work ethic to studios in Toronto, NYC, and Miami. Elijah comes from a musical and poetic family and is the only singer he knows of amongst the$ clan. The$ Brooklyn native who is in his early 20’s has performed at times on stage for talent and fashion shows and is eager to blossom further with future performances to the delight of his burgeoning fan base, drawing from within what inspires as he makes his promising way into the industry of today’s music.
